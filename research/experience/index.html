Project

The Role and Mechanism of Lomitapide in the Activation of Inflammasomes

Institute of Immunology, University of Science and Technology of ChinaJuly 2021 - May 2022
Advisor: Prof. Rongbin Zhou & Prof. Wei Jiang

  • Confirmed lomitapide as an agonist in the activation of inflammasomes, and determined the appropriate concentration and time to activate inflammasomes.
  • Indicated that lomitapide activates NLRP3 inflammasomes by comparing the amount of IL-1β released by Nlrp3-/- KO macrophages with that released by WT macrophages.
  • Discovered that the activation of inflammasomes diminishes with the concentration of extracellular K+ increasing, implying the dependence of activation on K+ efflux.
  • Demonstrated mitochondrial lesions in macrophages treated with lomitapide, relating the activation of inflammasomes to the dysfunction of mitochondria.
  • May contribute to the discovery of new targets for lomitapide, and help to explain its adverse effects including fatty acid accumulation in the liver.
